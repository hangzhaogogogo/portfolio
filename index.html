About Me

I am an applied economist, educator, and data scientist with a Ph.D. in Agricultural and Resource Economics from the University of Connecticut. My work combines causal inference, machine learning, econometrics, and data visualization to study questions in health, education, labor, and public policy.

I currently serve as a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Department of Business and Economics and the Department of Computer and Information Science at Allegheny College. I teach courses in economics, statistics, data science, databases, discrete mathematics, and quantitative methods.

My research focuses on translating complex data into clear and actionable evidence. Recent projects examine the effects of health and education policies, student retention, generative artificial intelligence in higher education, aging, food insecurity, and predictive analytics.

What I Do

  • Causal Inference & Policy Evaluation

    I use difference-in-differences, event studies, fixed-effects models, and other empirical methods to evaluate the effects of public policies and institutional changes.

  • Machine Learning & Predictive Analytics

    I develop interpretable prediction models for student retention, institutional planning, health outcomes, and data-informed decision-making.

  • Data Analysis & Visualization

    I transform complex administrative, survey, and longitudinal data into clear statistical evidence, visualizations, dashboards, and research findings.

  • Teaching & Curriculum Development

    I design applied courses that connect economics, statistics, programming, artificial intelligence, and real-world problem-solving.
